## Microsoft Advertising Relevance & Quality Standards

Microsoft Advertising requires that ads be clearly relevant to the keywords they target and to the landing page they link to. The ad headline and description must accurately describe the product or service being offered and align with the user's search intent. Ads that use unrelated keywords to capture traffic — keyword stuffing or bait-and-switch tactics — are disapproved. Landing pages must deliver on the promise of the ad: if the ad promotes a specific product at a specific price, that product and price must be prominent and accurate on the destination page.

Editorial quality standards require that ad text avoid excessive punctuation, capitalization, or symbols used as attention-grabbing gimmicks. Repetitive words, phone numbers in headlines (unless using call extensions), and generic non-descriptive copy are not permitted. The display URL must be truthful and correspond to the actual landing page domain. Ads may not use "trick-to-click" language that misleads users into thinking they need to click for a reason unrelated to the offer (e.g., fake system warnings).

Landing page quality is factored into Ad Quality Score, which influences ad rank and cost-per-click. Pages that load slowly, have thin or low-quality content, require unnecessary personal information before showing the offer, or are primarily designed to push users to another site will receive lower quality scores. Microsoft reserves the right to disapprove ads or lower quality scores for landing pages that provide a poor user experience, even if the ad copy itself is technically compliant.
