## Google Ads Prohibited Practices

Google prohibits advertisers from abusing the ad network in ways that harm users or Google's systems. This includes misrepresentation — ads that deceive users about the advertiser's identity, the product being sold, or any material aspect of the offer. Advertisers may not omit key information about pricing, fees, or terms that would affect a user's decision to click.

Data collection and use must be transparent and compliant with Google's policies. Ads may not collect user data through deceptive means, and landing pages must not install malware, adware, or engage in drive-by downloads. Advertisers may not use Google's platforms to engage in phishing — impersonating reputable organizations to steal credentials or personal information.

Google prohibits "malicious or unwanted software" distributed through ads, meaning landing pages or promoted downloads must not install software without clear user consent. Ads may not engage in "ad fraud" practices, such as artificially inflating clicks or impressions. Advertisers who repeatedly or severely violate these practices face permanent account suspension.
