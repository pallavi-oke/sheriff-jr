## Microsoft Advertising Disallowed Content

Microsoft Advertising prohibits ads for products or services that are illegal in the targeted location, including ads for illegal drugs, counterfeit goods, and unauthorized weapons. Ads that facilitate illegal activities — such as identity theft, hacking, or fraud — are banned. Content that promotes or depicts violence, terrorism, or harm to people or animals is not allowed under any circumstances.

Ads containing adult or sexually explicit content are prohibited by default. This includes nudity, suggestive imagery, and content targeting sexual services. Tobacco products (cigarettes, cigars, smokeless tobacco) are globally prohibited. Ads for e-cigarettes and vaping products are not allowed in most markets, regardless of local legality. Similarly, ads for illegal firearms, ammunition, and related accessories are disallowed, though licensed firearm dealers may advertise in permitted jurisdictions with category approval.

Microsoft also prohibits misleading ads, including those that impersonate reputable brands, make false factual claims, or use deceptive tactics to obtain clicks. Ads that promote multi-level marketing schemes with unrealistic income promises, or that target vulnerable populations (such as those in financial distress) with predatory offers, are not allowed. Any advertiser found running disallowed content may face immediate ad suspension and potential permanent account termination.
