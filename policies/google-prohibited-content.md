## Google Ads Prohibited Content

Google absolutely prohibits ads that promote counterfeit goods — products bearing a brand's logo or trademark without authorization. Ads may not promote dangerous products or services, including those that facilitate serious harm to people or animals (e.g., illegal weapons, drugs, explosives). Content that enables dishonest behavior, such as hacking tools, fake documents, or academic cheating services, is also banned outright.

Ads may not contain inappropriate content, including content that is sexually explicit, profane, or that shocks or disgusts users without legitimate purpose. Content targeting individuals or groups with hate speech — defined as promoting hatred or discrimination based on race, ethnicity, religion, gender, sexual orientation, national origin, disability, or age — is strictly prohibited. Similarly, ads must not threaten, harass, or bully individuals.

Ads may not facilitate the sale or use of illegal drugs, prescription drugs without proper authorization, or unapproved supplements making medical claims. Content related to tobacco, e-cigarettes, or recreational drugs is prohibited regardless of legality in a given jurisdiction. Google also prohibits ads that endanger child safety in any form, including any content that sexualizes minors or enables access to them by predators.
