## Google Ads Editorial & Technical Requirements

Google's editorial standards require that ads meet a minimum bar for quality and professionalism. Ad text must use proper grammar, capitalization, punctuation, and spacing — excessive capitalization (e.g., ALL CAPS in headlines), repeated punctuation ("!!!"), or gimmick symbols are not allowed. Ads must be written in the primary language of the target country and must clearly relate to the product or service being promoted.

Ads must not be vague or unclear about what is being offered. Generic calls-to-action such as "Click here" are discouraged, and ad copy must not make superlative claims ("best in the world") without substantiation. Prices, discounts, and promotional claims shown in ad text must match what is available on the landing page. Trademarks may only be used in ad text by the trademark owner or with explicit permission; using a competitor's trademark as a keyword trigger may be restricted depending on the jurisdiction.

Technically, ads must link to a functioning landing page that loads correctly across devices. The display URL shown in the ad must match the domain of the actual landing page. Landing pages may not redirect users unexpectedly, open multiple pop-ups, or interfere with the browser's back button. Ads that are technically broken — invalid URLs, missing creative assets — are disapproved automatically.
