## Google Ads Restricted Content

Certain categories of content are allowed on Google Ads but only under specific conditions and with prior authorization. Alcohol advertising is permitted in many countries but must comply with local laws, must not target minors, and must not portray excessive or irresponsible drinking. Gambling and games of chance require country-specific certification from Google before running, and ads must not be shown to users under the legal gambling age in their jurisdiction.

Healthcare and pharmaceutical ads are heavily restricted. Prescription drug ads require pre-approval and are only permitted in a handful of countries (the US, Canada, New Zealand, and Brazil). Non-prescription supplements may run but cannot make unsubstantiated health claims. Addiction treatment services, abortion services, and clinical trial recruitment each have their own approval requirements. Financial products such as payday loans, debt management, and credit cards must comply with country-specific disclosure requirements and may require pre-certification.

Adult content (non-explicit) is allowed only in jurisdictions where it is legal and must be opted into via account settings, ensuring it is not shown to minors. Political ads require identity verification and vary in allowed formats by country. Any advertiser wishing to run ads in these restricted categories must apply for authorization, demonstrate compliance with local law, and accept Google's supplemental terms for that vertical.
